Sundarbans Tourism
One of the largest active deltas in the world, the Sundarbans, named after the Sundari mangrove trees, is shared by India and Bangladesh. It’s famous for its 40,000 sq. km mangrove forest, 260+ bird species, and the Royal Bengal tiger. Read More

Sundarbans National Park is around 110 km south of Kolkata. It is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a Biosphere Reserve, home to rare species like the Royal Bengal tiger, salt-water crocodile, and King Crabs. With over 64 types of mangroves and more than 400 tigers, the area is known for its rich biodiversity. Top spots include Hiron Point, Katka Viewpoint, and the mudflats of Chargheri Char for coastal trekking.

Best Time to Visit:

September to March — ideal for pleasant weather. Avoid peak summer due to heat.

Where to Stay:

Gosaba — the largest inhabited island offering unique tourist accommodations.

Things to Do:

Boat safaris, bird-watching at Sajnekhali Bird Sanctuary, visiting the Crocodile Project, and night safaris to see glowing phytoplankton.
